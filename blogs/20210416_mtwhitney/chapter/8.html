Chapter 8: 5 am

so i guess the best way to preface this section is to first tell you all the proposed plan. we went to bed the night before at around 7 pm? p early, right? good for us. and so the plan was to:

  • wake up at 5 am
  • get ready
  • trek to the summit
  • do the summit
  • come down from summit
  • trek back to base camp

let me tell you how much of that i did in between some pretty photos of sunrise

did i wake up at 5 am?

yes.

did i get ready?

well, no. kinda. but no. so in the below photo, i do look ready to go BUT THERE WAS NO WAY I WAS GOING OUT THERE. i woke up intensely dehydrated like my head was throbbing, the earth was spinning. and it also didnt help that i felt like i barely slept the night before cuz of the headache. and so this photo where i do look ready to adventure with them is actaully me trying to help them get to their breakfast that i accidentally locking away by locking the bear cannister

plus snow. it was all/mostly snow the rest of the way. uh. yea. i dont snow well. maybe next time when i dont wake up dead from dehydration. also fun fact. we then checked the camelbak i was carrying and it can hold 3L of water. in 3 miles of trekking and however much elevation gain, in total, I barely drank .5L of water. so yeah.. makes sense while i look the way i do in all these photos

so, let's consult the plan again...

  • did i wake up at 5 am? - yes
  • did i get ready? - involuntarily
  • did i trek to the summit? - heck no
  • did i do the summit? - in my dreams
  • did i come down from summit? - i was high, so maybe
  • did i trek back to base camp? - does it count if i never left?

anyways here's me looking completely dejected holding the mountain house pack of granola which to make matters worst, i added too much water too

but not to fret! our healthy crew members are off! Jacob and Al tackle the snow and the last hurrah to the summit! woo! leaving a little later than expected at 7 am.
